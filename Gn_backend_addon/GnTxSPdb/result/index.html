Introduction

GnTxSPdb is an integrated database curating various types of secreted proteins in Gram-negative bacteria. Currently, the annotated proteins are experimentally validated or their close homologs. For each secreted protein, annotated information included the basic information about the gene and protein, secretion-related knowledge, distribution and evolution, structure, known function, co-expression networks, regulatory circuits, protein-protein interaction networks, and others. Users can browse the proteins by one of the two modes, taxon-based or secretion machinery-based mode. Taxon-based analysis on secreted proteins could facilitate secretome studies on individual bacterial strains or between-strain comparison on the total secreted proteins. Secretion machinery-based analysis assists deep investigation and understanding on the properties, evolution and secretion mechanisms of a specific type of secreted proteins. Another database (GnTxSSdb) curating protein secretion systems in Gram-negative bacteria, and a one-stop system (GnTxSPpp) predicting secreted proteins in Gram-negative bacteria, were developed synchronously and cross-referenced.

GnTxSPdb V1.0 annotated the comprehensive list of protein secretome for 8000 Gram-negative bacterial strains, covering 20 phyla, 100 genera and 500 species. The secretion pathways specify those spanning outer membrane at least and mediating the translocation of a whole or partial substrate toward extracellular space. The categories of secreted proteins include types 1 ~ 10, and some non-classical pathways.
